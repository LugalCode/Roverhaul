SECTION 01

High Concept

ROVERHAUL is a dark atmospheric idle exploration game set on a decaying alien world where the remnants of a once-advanced biomechanical civilization have fused with the planet itself.

The player is the sole survivor of a catastrophic crash landing aboard a failing colony ship. Unable to survive directly outside the vessel, they rely on a Remote Operative Vehicle for Environmental Reconnaissance — the ROVER — to venture into the wasteland beyond the airlock.

Every expedition pushes slightly further into the unknown. Every resource unlocks another upgrade. Every biome reveals fragments of something ancient that may never truly have died.

The experience combines idle progression systems, modular customization, environmental storytelling, and infinite side-scrolling exploration into a long-term survival journey centered around persistence, discovery, and adaptation.

SECTION 02

Core Design Philosophy

Progression

The player should always have a visible next objective. New systems, upgrades, facilities, and discoveries remain consistently within reach.

Automation

Earlier mechanics become automated over time, allowing focus to shift toward more advanced systems rather than repetitive micromanagement.

Active vs Idle

Active play offers efficiency improvements without punishing idle players. Engagement is rewarding rather than mandatory.

SECTION 03

Setting & Atmosphere

The world of ROVERHAUL is bleak, immense, and deeply unsettling. Endless industrial ruins merge with alien organic growth. Vast structures of flesh-metal architecture rise from poisoned landscapes beneath collapsing skies.

Environmental storytelling replaces traditional exposition. History is uncovered through fragmented archives, abandoned facilities, alien language fragments, impossible megastructures, and visual implication.

SECTION 04

Core Gameplay Loop

DEPLOY → EXPLORE → GATHER → PROCESS → UPGRADE → REPEAT

Expeditions begin with rover configuration and deployment. The rover traverses the endless side-scrolling world automatically while harvesting resources, mapping terrain, and encountering environmental points of interest.

Players may remain passive or directly assist the rover through movement boosts, harvesting optimization, and terrain navigation support.

Once power is depleted or the rover is recalled, emergency retrieval systems pull the unit back to the colony ship where resources are processed into upgrades, facility repairs, and research progression.

SECTION 05

Rover Customization

The rover functions as the heart of progression. Rather than simple numerical upgrades, it evolves through meaningful modular loadout decisions.

Chassis Frame

Determines payload capacity, compatibility, and structural durability.

Locomotion System

Controls speed, terrain traversal, traction, and hazard resistance.

Front Utility Mount

Supports drills, excavation tools, scanners, plows, and specialized equipment.

Rear Module

Provides storage, portable processing systems, and utility support.

Power Core

Determines energy capacity, efficiency, recharge speed, and advanced system support.

Armor Shell

Protective plating resistant to environmental and biomechanical hazards.

As progression advances, the rover gradually transforms from industrial machinery into something partially organic — adapted to survive the alien world through biomechanical integration.

SECTION 07

Exploration & World Structure

The world is infinite, but not procedurally meaningless. Every biome contains distinct terrain, hazards, structures, and narrative implications.

Exploration reveals abandoned megastructures, alien remains, impossible architecture, and biomechanical landmarks that act as progression milestones throughout the game.

The endless horizon is not empty. It is watching.
